Expand a panel to fullscreen

End-to-end directions for focusing a panel by keyboard and blowing it up to fill the screen. Variant D is the selected direction; A/B/C are kept below as exploration history. Every frame is live — hover a frame, then press 1–6 to focus a panel, press F to maximize the focused panel, and Esc to restore. Mouse users can click a panel to focus it and click the ⤢ icon in its header to expand.

How focus & expand work (shared across variants)

All three directions share the same two affordances — a focus hotkey badge surfaced in the panel header, and a Maximize icon. They differ only in what the expanded view covers.

Variant D — Immersive, tabs become the top bar ✓ selected direction

The immersive full-bleed of Variant B, but the covered top-bar strip isn't blank — it becomes the maximized section's header. If that section holds several panels, their tabs sit along the top so you can switch between them without leaving the maximized view. The far-left nav rail stays put; the workspace banner is replaced (not just hidden) by the section's tab strip + an exit control on the right.

Variant A — Maximize in grid · explored, superseded by D

The panel grows to fill the panel grid only. The nav rail and the top workspace bar stay visible, so your branch, diff summary, and panel toggles never leave the screen. Least disruptive — everything else just hides behind the maximized panel.

Variant B — Immersive full-bleed · explored, not chosen

The panel covers everything except the far-left nav rail — including the top bar. A single floating "Esc to exit" pill is the only chrome. Maximum pixels for the work itself; closest to a per-panel zen mode.

Variant C — Spotlight overlay · explored, not chosen

The panel lifts into a large centered card over a dimmed backdrop. The rest of the layout stays in place behind the dim (nav rail beside it, fully lit). It's a modal "peek": press Esc or click the dimmed area to drop straight back. Nothing reflows.
